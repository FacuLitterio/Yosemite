How does aging affect our body’s collagen?

Collagen is among the most abundant proteins in the body. It actually makes up for 30% of all the protein in your body, and most of the protein in your skin – 75% total.

Collagen is a protein made up of long chains of linked amino acids. It’s actually the most abundant source of protein in our entire body. We can think of collagen as a foundational building block, providing strength and structure for our connective tissues.

In our adult years, our bodies begin to produce less collagen. This process begins when we are around the age of 30 and accelerates in our 40s – leading to the inevitable visual and noticeable signs of aging. Collagen loss is a natural process, but other factors such as exposure to UV, pollution, and lifestyle choices can lead to early or more intense signs of aging.

What happens when we age?

The importance of Collagen in our bodies is huge but, naturally, as we age it starts to decline affecting our skin, nails, hair, joints, bones and muscles.

Muscles

As we age the amount of collagen in the connective tissue declines, this tissue is responsible for bundling muscle fibers into a strong and functioning muscle. It is only natural to get older but our muscle´s strength and function decreases, affecting our balance, gait, and overall mobility.

Bones

Our bones get weaker as collagen levels and the content of minerals decrease. With time, this causes them to become more fragile and breakable.

Joints

Aging wears and tears on the joint cartilage, reducing their well-functioning. This results in joint discomfort, making it harder for us to stay active as we age.

Nails

Our nails require a variety of nutrients including protein, vitamins and minerals to stay strong and healthy. As the years go by, they start lacking these nutrients.

Hair

As hair grows out of skin, it is important to understand that the aging of our skin also affects our hair. Collagen contributes to the elasticity and strength of our dermis. With age, our bodies become less efficient at producing collagen and replenishing cells in the dermis.

Skin

A decline in your body’s production of collagen, elastin and hyaluronic acid is what causes dry, dull, wrinkled, and sagging skin. Both internal and external factors damage skin and have an effect on its appearance.
