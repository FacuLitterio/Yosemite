What causes brain fog?

Brain fog can show up in a variety of ways. You might be unable to concentrate for long enough—on work tasks, conversations, or even on the words you’re reading right now. You may have difficulty making up your mind, small decisions are a big deal, you need more coffee to focus, more snacks to stay awake. Brain fog can be a symptom of a nutrient deficiency, sleep disorder, bacterial overgrowth from overconsumption of sugar, depression, or even a thyroid condition.

Some of the most common brain fog causes

Hormonal changes

Impaired sleep

Diet deficiencies and food sensitivities

Stress

Depression and anxiety

Heavy metal exposure
