What happens when we get sick?

Your immune system is your body's safeguard, when threatened with an illness it releases white blood cells to fight the infection. This battle between virus and white blood cells is the cause of symptoms and signs of sickness. While there are millions of different symptoms for illnesses, there are a few common signs that we would all be aware of but might not know why they happen, such as: runny nose, persistent cough, sore throat.

Which are the factors that affect our immune system?

Lifestyle

It has been proven that a sedentary lifestyle can prevent our immune system from functioning correctly. Inactivity can lead to an impaired immune system, inflammation, and other diseases. Also, too much strenuous exercise, called overtraining syndrome, can be debilitating for the body and make it more vulnerable to infection.

Stress and depression

Stress, anxiety, depression and negative emotions can weaken our defenses in our organism increasing the chances of catching an inflammatory or infectious disease. All of these negative feelings or emotions affect our nervous system balance directly which is strictly connected with our immune system.

Poor diet

Our defenses can be affected by a diet poor in nutrients since we are what we eat and what we eat affects the maintenance of our immune system directly. It all goes both ways, it can be a deficit or an excess of nutrients like vitamins, iron or minerals that can affect the correct functioning of your organism.

Pollution

Pollution affects our bodies whether it is through water, air or food. Studies have shown that when we expose ourselves to polluted air the function of our regulatory T cells decreases. These cells are a part of the immune system that focuses on specific foreign particles and these cells play a critical part in immunity to foreign substances.

Alcohol and cigarettes

Cytokines are substances naturally found in the body, which are released as part of the immune system’s response to invading pathogens. Inhaling tobacco prompts an increase in cytokine activity, worsening the inflammation at the site of infection. Gut microorganisms which are naturally found in the body are essential in fighting off infections and drinking alcohol can affect these organisms.
